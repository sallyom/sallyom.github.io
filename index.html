  • OpenClaw: How the Movement Becomes a Standard

    • Apr 11, 2026
    • 4 minute read

    I’ve spent a decade in open source. I’ve seen how projects evolve from idea to product, how communities form and fracture, and how entire industries shift when people decide to build together. I know the pattern, but I’ve never seen it happen this fast. OpenClaw’s adoption has been unusually fast, and that changes the calculus for everyone building nearby.

    If you’re thinking about building “the next OpenClaw,” I encourage you to consider a different framing. OpenClaw may have started the Claw movement, but what matters now is not whether every new effort tries to replace it. It’s whether people recognize what is actually forming around it.
